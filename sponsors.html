Partner Charities

The Ethiopian Knowledge and Transfer Society was founded in 2001 by Engineers Berhane Abate and Syioum Gebeyehou. Their group focuses on mobilizing educational resources to support Ethiopian development. To date, they have distributed over 1.5 million books and thousands of computers throughout Ethiopia!

Computer Aid International is a leading organization dedicated to ICT (information and computer technology) proliferation in developing nations. Their projects not only involve bringing hardware to under-developed regions, but providing training to the local population to ensure their long-term sustainability.

World Computer Exchange, founded by Timothy Anderson of the Kenndy School of Government at Harvard University, is a non-profit organization dedicated to importing unwanted but perfectly functional technology from Canada and the United States to developing nations. With an extensive network of over 1000 volunteers and 900 partner organizations in 81 countries around the world, WCE has to date shipped over 30,000 computers, as well as installed technology and learning centres to provide the basics of computer literacy.
