Millions of students and entrepreneurs have no access to the technology necessary for success in the 21st century.

Our organization inspires citizens of developing nations to make a positive difference in their communities. We focus on providing free technological resources to schools, entrepreneurs, and community groups.

The Technology Divide 101

With the exponential proliferation of information technology in first-world countries during the late 20th and early 21st centuries, hundreds of millions of people now have access to vast amounts of online data.

The internet is a powerful tool that is taken for granted by many people in Canada; it is used frequently for social networking, emails, casual and academic research, and shopping (to name a few). But while Canadians enjoy the benefits of technology that has greatly raised our living standards, billions of people in far-away countries have no such privilege.

Africa, South and Central America, the Middle-East, Central and South-Asia and the Pacific are traditionally underdeveloped when it comes to the field of information-technology. Basic infrastructure such as computers and even internet connections are not available in many communities; as a result, well over half the world’s population has no computer literacy skills and is cut off from a huge amount of information.

What We Do

At the Waterloo Technology Foundation, we are committed to reversing this trend by supporting worthy non-profit organizations working to integrate technology into developing communities on a grassroots level. Every person in the world, no matter who they are and where they are from, deserves the opportunity to use and benefit from technological resources.

The foundation, purely and online resource, serves to connect Canadians with worthy organizations from around the world working hard to bring information technology to developing regions (please see a list below). We gratefully accept donations of any amount, which are routed directly to our partner organizations for use in their projects. Contacting/making a donation directly to our partners is also encouraged.

Through our partners, the foundation’s goal is to target three main criteria that are central to community development.

Schools train each nation’s next generation of workers...a key to future prosperity is ensuring that students are comfortable with using technology. These skills not only make them useful community assets, but greatly boost employment potential.

Hard-working buisnesspeople with ideas breath fresh life and diversity into local economies. These initiatives require technical support, and ensuring that entrepreneurs receive proper training to market their business can mean the difference between success and failure.

Centres such as libraries and post-offices serve as meeting points for rural communities. Connecting these hubs with technology will allow locals to access basic internet services, and therefore improve their ability to communicate with the world!.
